#### Key Concepts Explained

**1. Definition and Types of Alternative Investments**
- Investments outside traditional stocks, bonds, cash
- Types covered:
  - Real Estate (REITs vs direct ownership)
  - Commodities (gold, oil, agricultural products)
  - Private Equity (investing in private companies)
  - Hedge Funds (sophisticated strategies, high minimums)
  - Collectibles (art, wine, rare cars)
  - Cryptocurrency (Bitcoin, Ethereum - controversial)

**2. Main Benefits of Alternatives**
- **Low correlation with traditional markets** = primary benefit
- Reduces overall portfolio volatility
- Provides diversification beyond stocks and bonds
- Some alternatives offer inflation protection (real estate, gold, commodities)
- Can smooth returns during market downturns

**3. Key Risks and Tradeoffs**
- **Liquidity Risk** - can take months/years to sell, lock-up periods common
- **High Fees** - often 1-2% management + 20% performance fees (hedge funds)
- **Less Transparency** - harder to value, less regulation than public markets
- **Higher Minimums** - often $100K-$1M+ minimum investment
- **Complexity** - requires specialized knowledge to evaluate
- **Over-allocation risk** - too many alternatives can cause problems

**4. Appropriate Allocation Guidelines**
- **CFP Recommendation:** 5-15% maximum for most clients
- Higher allocations (40-50%) create excessive liquidity risk
- Must balance diversification benefits against fees and liquidity constraints

**5. Specific Alternative Investment Characteristics**

**Real Estate:**
- REITs (publicly traded) = LIQUID, trade like stocks, 4% typical dividend yield
- Direct property = ILLIQUID, months to sell, requires management
- Benefits: Income + inflation hedge + appreciation potential
- Risks: Property values can crash, vacancies, maintenance costs

**Commodities:**
- Gold = inflation hedge, negative correlation with stocks, NO INCOME
- Oil/agriculture = volatile, affected by supply/demand, weather, geopolitics
- Investment methods: Futures, ETFs, physical ownership
- Benefits: Inflation protection, diversification
- Risks: No income, high volatility, storage costs (physical)

**Private Equity / Venture Capital:**
- Invest in private companies pre-IPO
- VERY illiquid - 7-10 year lock-ups common
- High minimums ($250K-$1M+)
- High risk, high potential return
- Only for sophisticated, wealthy investors

**Hedge Funds:**
- Sophisticated strategies: short selling, leverage, derivatives
- "2 and 20" fee structure = 2% management + 20% of profits
- Lock-up periods: 1-3 years common
- Accredited investors only ($1M+ net worth typically)
- Mixed track record - many underperform after fees

**Collectibles:**
- Highly illiquid, subjective valuation
- No income, high storage/insurance costs
- Not recommended for serious portfolios
- More hobby than investment

  - Income needs
  - Risk tolerance
  - Liquidity requirements
  - Tax situation
