### 1. Education Needs Analysis - Four-Step Process

**Concepts Taught**:

**Step 1: Estimate Future Education Costs**
- Current costs (2025): Public in-state $28K, Out-of-state $46K, Private $60K per year
- Education inflation: 5-6% per year (higher than general inflation!)
- Formula: Future Cost = Current Cost √ó (1 + inflation)^years
- Typical duration: 4 years undergraduate

**Step 2: Determine Time Horizon**
- Time horizon affects monthly contribution needs and asset allocation
- Longer time = more aggressive allocation (80-90% stocks)
- As college approaches, shift to conservative (bonds, cash)
- Age-based asset allocation similar to retirement planning

**Step 3: Calculate Funding Gap**
- Gap = Total Future Cost - FV of Current Savings
- Must grow current savings to see what they'll become
- Gap represents what needs to be saved through monthly contributions

**Step 4: Calculate Required Monthly Savings**
- Use FV Annuity formula (from B.12) solved for PMT
- PMT = FV √ó r / [(1 + r)^n - 1]
- Apply monthly conversion (r/12, n√ó12) if saving monthly

### 3. Key Factors Affecting Education Needs Analysis

**Factors Taught**:

1. **Type of School**
   - Public in-state (cheapest) vs private (most expensive)
   - Community college first 2 years strategy (cost-saving)

2. **Number of Children**
   - Twins = 4 years of double payments (overlapping)
   - Need to save MUCH more monthly or start earlier
   - Financial aid benefit: 2 in college can increase aid eligibility

3. **Financial Aid Considerations**
   - Expected Family Contribution (EFC)
   - Merit vs need-based scholarships
   - Not all assets count equally in aid calculations

4. **Funding Sources Mix**
   - CFP Rule of Thumb: 1/3 savings, 1/3 income, 1/3 loans/scholarships
   - Diversification reduces risk

5. **Flexibility and Assumptions**
   - Scholarships are uncertain - plan conservatively
   - Child may choose cheaper school
   - 529 plans have rules if child doesn't attend college

6. **Competing Goals - MOST CRITICAL**
   - **Retirement ALWAYS comes before education**
   - "You can borrow for college, but you can't borrow for retirement"
   - Financially independent parents reduce burden on children
