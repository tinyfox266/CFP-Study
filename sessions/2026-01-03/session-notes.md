#### Concepts Taught - Retirement Needs Analysis Framework

**1. Replacement Ratio Concept:**
- Definition: Retirement Income Ã· Pre-Retirement Income
- Typical range: 70-85% (not 100% because certain expenses disappear)
- Expenses that go away: retirement savings, commuting, work clothes, sometimes mortgage
- 70-75%: High earners, mortgage paid off
- 75-80%: Average situation
- 80-85%: Lower earners, ongoing expenses

**2. Two Calculation Methods:**

**Method 1 - Expense Method (Bottom-Up, More Accurate):**
- Step 1: Estimate annual retirement expenses (housing, food, healthcare, travel, other)
- Step 2: Adjust for inflation to retirement age
- Step 3: Calculate total retirement needs (annual expenses Ã— years in retirement)
- Step 4: Identify income sources (Social Security, pension, part-time work)
- Step 5: Calculate funding gap (total needs - income sources)

**Method 2 - Replacement Ratio Method (Top-Down, Quicker):**
- Step 1: Determine appropriate replacement ratio (70-85%)
- Step 2: Calculate annual retirement income needed (pre-retirement income Ã— ratio)
- Step 3: Same as Expense Method (Steps 2-5)

**3. Key Considerations:**

**Inflation:**
- General inflation: ~2-3% per year
- Healthcare inflation: ~5-6% per year (HIGHER!)
- Must use real returns (Nominal - Inflation) from B.12
