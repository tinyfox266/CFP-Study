### PART 3: Debt Consolidation & Refinancing

**Baseline Assessment - What is consolidation?**
- **Student's answer:** "Combine multiple debts into one debt. For example, borrow from one credit card to pay off other cards."
- **Assessment:** EXACTLY RIGHT! Even gave balance transfer example!

**Key Concepts Taught:**

**Method 1: Balance Transfer Credit Cards**
- 0% intro APR (12-18 months)
- 3-5% transfer fee
- Risk: High interest after intro period if not paid off
- Requires good credit score

**Method 2: Personal Consolidation Loans**
- Fixed rate (8-15%), lower than credit cards (18-25%)
- Fixed term (2-5 years) = forced discipline
- Origination fee (1-5%)

**Method 3: Home Equity Loan/HELOC** ⚠️
- Lowest interest (5-9%) because secured
- **CRITICAL RISK:** Converts unsecured → secured debt
- If can't pay = LOSE HOUSE
- CFP generally does NOT recommend

**Consolidation vs Refinancing:**
- Consolidation: Multiple debts → One new loan
- Refinancing: One debt → One new loan (better terms)

### PART 4: Debt-to-Income Ratios (DTI)

**Baseline Assessment - Intuitive DTI Threshold:**
- Scenario: Income $6,000, debts total $2,100/month (35% DTI)
- **Student's intuitive answer:** "Debt burden is not heavy. If expenses > 50% of income, burden is heavy because little money for savings. If lose income, life becomes tight."
- **Assessment:** EXCELLENT LOGICAL REASONING!
  - Understood relationship between debt, savings capacity, and financial resilience
  - 50% threshold too high for CFP standards, but logic was perfect

**Formal Teaching:**

**DTI Calculation:**
- DTI = Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income
- Debt = mortgage, car, student loan, credit card minimums (NOT utilities, food, etc.)

**CFP/Lending Standards:**
- **Housing Ratio (Front-end):** PITI ÷ Income ≤ 28%
- **Total Debt Ratio (Back-end):** All debt ÷ Income ≤ 36%

**Benchmarks:**
- 0-28%: Excellent (easy loan approval)
- 28-36%: Acceptable (moderate risk)
- 36-43%: High (difficult approval)
- 43%+: Too high (usually denied)
