### PART 2: Business Cycle Phases

**Key Concepts Taught:**

**Four Phases:**

1. **EXPANSION** (Growth Phase)
   - GDP rising, unemployment falling
   - Consumers confident, spending more
   - Businesses hiring, investing
   - Stock market generally rising
   - Duration: Typically 4-6 years

2. **PEAK** (Top of Cycle)
   - Economy at maximum output
   - Unemployment very low (3-4%)
   - Inflation starting to rise (economy "overheating")
   - Warning sign for potential contraction

3. **CONTRACTION/RECESSION** (Decline Phase)
   - GDP falling for 2+ consecutive quarters
   - Unemployment rising
   - Consumer spending drops
   - Business profits decline
   - Stock market falling
   - Duration: Typically 6-18 months

4. **TROUGH** (Bottom of Cycle)
   - Economy at lowest point
   - Unemployment peaked
   - Often best time to invest (stocks cheap)
   - Recovery typically follows

### PART 3: Inflation - Types and Effects

**Key Concepts Taught:**

**Definition:**
- General increase in prices over time
- Decrease in purchasing power of money
- Measured by CPI (Consumer Price Index)
- Fed targets ~2% inflation (healthy)

**Types of Inflation:**

1. **Moderate Inflation (2-4%)** ‚úÖ Healthy
   - Encourages spending/investing
   - Economy growing steadily
   - CFP Impact: Adjust retirement projections for ~3% inflation

2. **High Inflation (5-10%)** ‚ö†Ô∏è Problematic
   - Purchasing power erodes quickly
   - Uncertainty increases
   - CFP Impact: Recommend inflation-protected investments (TIPS, real estate, commodities)

3. **Hyperinflation (>10%, often >50%)** üö® Crisis
   - Currency becomes nearly worthless
   - Economic collapse
   - Rare in developed countries

4. **Deflation (Negative inflation)** üîª Dangerous
   - Prices falling
   - Sounds good but actually BAD for economy
   - People delay purchases ‚Üí businesses suffer ‚Üí unemployment rises

**Inflation Impact on Financial Planning:**

**Purchasing Power:**
- At 3% inflation: $100 today = $74 purchasing power in 10 years
- At 7% inflation: $100 today = $50 purchasing power in 10 years

**Investment Selection:**
- Good inflation protection: Stocks, real estate, TIPS, commodities
- Bad inflation protection: Cash, fixed-rate bonds (lose purchasing power)

**Real Return = Nominal Return - Inflation**

### PART 4: Fiscal Policy vs Monetary Policy

**Key Concepts Taught:**

**FISCAL POLICY (Government Spending & Taxes)**

**Who controls:** Congress and President

**Tools:**
1. Government spending (infrastructure, defense, social programs)
2. Taxation (income tax, corporate tax)

**During Recession ‚Üí STIMULATE:**
- Increase spending (stimulus checks, infrastructure)
- Cut taxes (people keep more money)
- Result: More money in economy ‚Üí demand increases ‚Üí recovery

**During Expansion/Inflation ‚Üí COOL DOWN:**
- Decrease spending
- Raise taxes
- Result: Less money in economy ‚Üí demand decreases ‚Üí inflation slows

---

**MONETARY POLICY (Interest Rates & Money Supply)**

**Who controls:** Federal Reserve (Independent from government!)

**Tools:**
1. Federal Funds Rate (interest rate banks charge each other)
2. Open Market Operations (buying/selling bonds)
3. Reserve Requirements

**During Recession ‚Üí STIMULATE:**
- Lower interest rates (make borrowing cheap)
- Buy bonds (inject money)
- Result: Cheap loans ‚Üí businesses invest ‚Üí consumers buy ‚Üí economy grows

**During Expansion/Inflation ‚Üí SLOW:**
- Raise interest rates (make borrowing expensive)
- Sell bonds (take money out)
- Result: Expensive loans ‚Üí less borrowing ‚Üí demand falls ‚Üí inflation slows

**Comparison:**

| Aspect | Fiscal Policy | Monetary Policy |
|--------|---------------|-----------------|
| Who | Congress/President | Federal Reserve |
| Tools | Spending & Taxes | Interest Rates & Money Supply |
| Speed | SLOW (laws take time) | FAST (Fed can act quickly) |
| Politics | Very political | Independent, less political |
